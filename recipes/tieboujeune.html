Thiéboudienne

Descriptions

Thiéboudiène, also written thiéboudienne or sometimes tieb bou dien is the most popular of the thieb.

Often abbreviated, wrongly, to tieb, it is commonly called "tiep" in Senegal or ceebu jën.

Ingredients

  • 4 beautiful sea bream
  • 200 ml peanut oil
  • 1 kg of broken rice (broken rice)
  • 6 tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato puree
  • 2 tbsp parsley
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 patates douces
  • 1 eggplant
  • 2 tbsp dried onions
  • 4 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 vegetable stock cubes
  • 4 to 6 bird's eye chilies
  • Salt
  • pepper

Steps

  1. First, prepare the filling for the sea bream. Mix the parsley, half the garlic and peppers, a vegetable stock cube, and pound everything in a mortar.
  2. Rinse and dry the previously emptied fish and stuff them with the mixture. Close the fish using toothpicks.
  3. In a casserole, heat the oil and fry the fish for 5 minutes on each side over medium heat.
  4. Lower the heat and add the remaining garlic and the remaining chiles, then the onion and cook again for 5 minutes.
  5. Then add the diced tomatoes and the tomato puree, add the remaining vegetable stock cube, the bay leaves and 10 cl of water, then simmer for about 20 minutes over low heat.
  6. Pendant ce temps coupez votre carotte en gros morceaux les carottes, l'aubergine et les patates douces.
  7. Add the vegetables to the pan and cook again for about 30 minutes.
  8. Then remove the vegetables and sea bream, then add the rice and about 1 liter of water, so that the latter largely covers the rice. Let cook for 20 minutes.
  9. Servez d'abord le riz, puis les légumes et enfin le poisson, dans chaque assiette.
