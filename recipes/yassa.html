Yassa

Decription

Yassa is a Senegalese dish made with fried onions and rice and which can be accompanied by meat marinated in lemon then fried or braised, chicken or fish.

It is a simple dish, quick to make and often appreciated by all thanks to its simple and economical basic ingredients.

Ingredients

  • Fried Onions
  • Rice
  • Meat
  • Lemon
  • Salt/pepper
  • bulb of garlic
  • sunflower oil

Steps

  1. Squeeze the 4 lemons to collect the juice then peel and slice two onions and crush the garlic clove. Cut the poultry into pieces. In a bowl, make the marinade for the Yassa chicken: mix half the oil with the juice of the 4 lemons, the crushed garlic clove and the sliced onions. Salt and pepper lightly.
  2. Arrange the chicken pieces in a dish and cover them with the marinade. Let them marinate in the refrigerator for 6 to 12 hours (ideally overnight). Mix from time to time so that all the chicken breasts are well impregnated with the marinade. Remove the chicken pieces from the marinade then lightly grill them over the coals, in the oven or in a pan.
  3. Next, peel and chop the remaining 3 onions. Chop and deseed the chilli. In a pot, put the rest of the oil and brown the rest of the onions. Pour the marinade. Then add the chilli, bay leaves and mustard. Mix then add the chicken pieces. Heat your Yassa chicken in the broth over low heat for 1 hour. Serve your chicken Yassa very hot and enjoy it immediately, with white rice or broken rice.
